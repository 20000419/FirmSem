# Frozen Result Scope

The Route-B release keeps five non-IID evidence strata separate:

- Development: 131 independently reviewed obligations, with 129 binary-scored
  rows after retaining two ambiguous labels outside the denominator.
- Untouched-37: 37 eligible obligations from previously unused projects,
  including five assertion-policy losses.
- New holdout v2: 72 eligible hard preserved obligations from six additional
  projects; this negative-only cohort measures eligibility and specificity,
  not positive transfer.
- P0-4: 13 independently adjudicated patch-paired incidents, represented by 52
  blinded build roles.
- P0-1: a bounded five-project mechanism matrix with 5,258 compiler profiles.
  Its 524 raw discrepancies contract to no confirmed pass-attributed,
  reachable, harmful loss under the bounded protocol, while 369 profiles
  remain unresolved and are not negatives.

The controlled compiler replay demonstrates that standards-conforming
optimization can change a security-policy outcome and produce an
attacker-controlled overwrite. It does not estimate field prevalence or claim
that every observed guard loss is optimizer-caused.

Assembly-only inference remains an identifiability stress test. Source-backed
obligation verification is the supported audit setting. The strata are not
pooled as IID records and do not estimate natural prevalence. See
`annotation_protocol.md` and the versioned frozen-evidence archive for exact
denominators, agreement statistics, claim ceilings, and acquisition
deviations.
